Hammurabi was the sixth king of the First Babylonian dynasty of the Amorite tribe, reigning from c.

1792 BC to c. 1750 BC. He was preceded by his father, Sin-Muballit, who abdicated due to failing health. During his reign, he conquered Elam and the city-states of Larsa, Eshnunna, and Mari.
